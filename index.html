§ About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Research Institute for Electronic Science (RIES), Hokkaido University, working with Prof. Hiromichi Ohta. I completed my Ph.D. in Materials Engineering at IIT Gandhinagar under Prof. Emila Panda, focused on chemical strain-induced structural and semiconductor-to-metal transitions in VO₂ thin films, combining RF magnetron sputtering with first-principles DFT (VASP) to understand and engineer dopant-driven phase transitions.

Before this, my M.Tech work at NIT Hamirpur examined how surfactant chemistry controls nanoparticle size, strain, and optical defect states in Cr₂O₃. Across both, the throughline is correlating synthesis conditions with structure, defects, and functional properties in inorganic oxide systems.

01 Research Focus

Doctoral Thesis

Chemical strain-induced structural and semiconductor-to-metal transition in VO₂ thin film. Substituted V⁴⁺ lattice sites with larger-radius, higher-valency cationic dopants to lower the transition temperature toward near-room-temperature levels, fabricated via RF magnetron sputtering, and explained with DFT on unstrained and strained M₁/R phases. Outcome: 2 Indian patents and 3 journal publications.

Master's Thesis

Effect of surfactants on the structural and optical properties of Cr₂O₃ nanoparticles. Modulated anionic (SDS) and cationic (CTAB) surfactant concentration during wet-chemical co-precipitation to study lattice strain, defect states, and the inverse relationship between Urbach energy and bandgap.

Thermochromic Smart Windows

Translating strain- and dopant-tuned VO₂ films into visibly transparent, highly stable heat-insulation coatings for energy-efficient buildings — supported by a Tata Steel MaterialNEXT4.0 student project grant.

Computational Modelling

First-principles DFT calculations (VASP, Quantum ESPRESSO) on thermodynamic stability and formation energies to elucidate substitution-induced phase transitions in VO₂.
